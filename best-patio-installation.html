HOA & Permits

HOA Approval and Permits for Patios in Northern Virginia

Most Northern Virginia homeowners live in HOA communities. Getting patio approval from your architectural review committee (ARC) is often the longest part of the project timeline — not the construction itself. P&L Home Group handles your ARC application as part of the design process.

HOA Approval Process in Loudoun County

Most Loudoun County HOAs — including those in Ashburn, Brambleton, Broadlands, Lansdowne, One Loudoun, Belmont Country Club, Willowsford, and Stone Ridge — require ARC approval before any patio installation. The typical approval process works as follows:

  • Application submission: We prepare your ARC package, including a site plan, material specifications, product samples, and 3D renderings of the proposed patio.
  • ARC review period: Most Loudoun County HOAs have a 30-day review window. Some newer communities review within 2 weeks. We build this timeline into your project schedule.
  • Conditions of approval: Some ARCs approve with conditions — specific material colors, setback distances from property lines, or landscape screening requirements. We address all conditions before construction begins.
  • Appeal process: If a first submission is rejected, we revise and resubmit. Most rejections are resolved within one revision cycle.

Permit Requirements for Patios in Virginia

Most paver patios at grade level — meaning the patio surface sits at or near existing ground level — do not require a building permit in Loudoun or Fairfax County. However, permits are required when:

  • The patio includes a retaining wall over 4 feet in height
  • The patio is structurally attached to the home's foundation
  • The project includes a deck, covered structure, or pergola
  • The patio is in a floodplain or critical resource area
  • The project includes a gas fire pit or outdoor kitchen with utilities

P&L Home Group researches permit requirements for your specific property and jurisdiction before the project begins. If a permit is required, we handle the application and scheduling of inspections. Permit fees are included in your fixed-price contract.

How much does a patio cost in Northern Virginia? +
Patio installation in Northern Virginia typically costs between $18 and $55 per square foot installed, depending on material and complexity. A 400 sq ft paver patio averages $22,000–$32,000. A 600 sq ft multi-level patio with steps and a fire pit averages $45,000–$70,000. P&L Home Group provides fixed-price quotes — the number we give you is the number you pay at completion.
How long does patio installation take? +
Most patio installations take 3–7 business days once materials arrive on site. A simple 400 sq ft paver patio typically takes 3–4 days. A large multi-level patio with stairs, walls, and a fire pit can take 7–12 days. Material lead times are typically 1–3 weeks from project approval.
Do I need a permit for a patio in Loudoun County? +
Most paver patios at grade level do not require a permit in Loudoun County. However, if the patio includes a retaining wall over 4 feet, is attached to the house foundation, or includes a deck or covered structure, a permit is required.
