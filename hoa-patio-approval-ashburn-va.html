HOA Patio Approval in Ashburn: What You Need to Know Before You Build

Ashburn homeowners who want to build a patio often discover — sometimes the hard way — that their HOA has significant say in what gets built and how. This isn't a bad thing. HOA architectural standards are part of what makes Brambleton, Broadlands, Ashburn Village, and Lansdowne look the way they do. But navigating the approval process takes time and preparation. Here's exactly how it works.

Which Ashburn Communities Require ARC Approval for Patios?

Almost all of them. The major Ashburn HOA communities that require architectural review committee (ARC) approval before patio construction include:

  • Brambleton Community Association
  • Broadlands Community Association
  • Ashburn Village Community Association
  • Lansdowne on the Potomac HOA
  • Belmont Country Club HOA
  • One Loudoun HOA
  • Belmont Greene HOA
  • Regency at Belmont HOA
  • Ryan Park at Brambleton HOA

If you're not sure whether your community requires ARC approval, check your HOA's community portal or contact your property manager. P&L Home Group looks this up for every client before the project begins.

What the ARC Application Typically Requires

Most Ashburn HOA ARC applications require the following documentation:

  • Site plan: A scaled drawing showing your property lot lines, existing structures, and the proposed patio footprint. Some communities require a survey; others accept a hand-drawn or CAD plan.
  • Material specifications: Paver brand, collection, color, and pattern. Most ARCs want to know specifically what you're installing — "Belgard Cambridge, color Harvest Blend, running bond pattern."
  • Product samples or photos: Many ARCs want to see what the product looks like. Manufacturer brochure pages or samples work.
  • 3D rendering or elevation sketch: Not always required, but increasingly common in premium communities. P&L prepares photorealistic 3D renderings for all clients, which we include in the ARC package.
  • Application form and fee: Varies by community. Most Ashburn HOAs charge $50–$200 for ARC review. A few charge nothing.

P&L Home Group prepares your complete ARC application package as part of our design process. You review it, sign the application form, and submit it to your HOA — or we submit it on your behalf if the HOA permits contractor submissions.

ARC Review Timeline in Ashburn

Most Ashburn HOAs have a 30-day review window from the date of application submission. Some communities review faster — Brambleton typically reviews within 2–3 weeks if the application is complete. A few review monthly at a scheduled ARC meeting, which can extend timelines to 4–6 weeks depending on when you submit relative to the meeting schedule.

We build the ARC timeline into your project schedule. If you want your patio installed by July 4th, we need to start the ARC process by mid-May at the latest.

Common Reasons ARC Applications Get Rejected

Most rejections come down to one of these issues:

  • Incomplete application: Missing a required document, photo, or form. We catch these before submission.
  • Material color not approved: Some communities restrict paver colors to neutral earth tones or require colors that match the home's exterior. If your first choice isn't approved, we usually have an alternative that is.
  • Setback violation: Patio footprint too close to the property line or fence. We design with HOA setback requirements in mind from the start.
  • Site plan issues: Inaccurate dimensions or missing property line information. We use accurate lot plat data in our site plans.
  • Scale or scope issues: A few communities have maximum coverage percentages for impervious surfaces. We check this before designing.

In my experience building patios across Ashburn's communities, a well-prepared initial application is approved roughly 85–90% of the time. The remaining 10–15% require one revision, which is usually resolved quickly.
