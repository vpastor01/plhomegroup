Belgard vs Techo-Bloc: Which Paver Brand is Better for Northern Virginia?

I get asked this question constantly during design consultations: Belgard or Techo-Bloc? Both brands make excellent pavers. Both are rated for Virginia's freeze-thaw climate. Both come with strong warranties. The right answer depends on your home's architecture, your aesthetic preferences, and — honestly — which specific products within each line you're looking at. Here's how I actually think about it.

The Short Version

Belgard is the larger manufacturer with broader distribution and more price points. Techo-Bloc is the premium brand with tighter tolerances and more European styling. Both are excellent. For most Loudoun County homes — Colonials, Craftsman, transitional styles — either brand delivers outstanding results. For contemporary and modern homes that want a very clean, refined look, Techo-Bloc's premium collections are hard to beat.

Here's a more detailed breakdown by what matters most.

Brand Overview

Belgard

Belgard is North America's largest paver manufacturer. Their product line is extensive — from entry-level residential pavers to premium designer series. They have regional manufacturing facilities that give them strong distribution throughout Northern Virginia. Belgard's most popular collections in this market include Cambridge, Mega Arbel, Olde Towne, and Urbana. They carry a limited lifetime warranty when installed by an authorized contractor.

Belgard's strength is breadth. Whatever look you're going for — traditional, transitional, contemporary, rustic — there's a Belgard product that fits. Their entry-level products are competitive on price while their premium lines hold up against anything Techo-Bloc makes.

Techo-Bloc

Techo-Bloc is a Canadian manufacturer that has built a reputation for premium products with very tight manufacturing tolerances. Their pavers have a more refined, consistent appearance straight out of the package — which you notice when you lay them. Popular Northern Virginia collections include Bel Air, Blu Grande, Origen, and Raffinato. Techo-Bloc carries a 25-year warranty on most products.

Techo-Bloc's strength is consistency and aesthetics. If you want a patio that looks like it could be in a design magazine — particularly a modern or transitional design — Techo-Bloc's premium collections deliver that. Their large-format products (Blu Grande, Raffinato) are among the most specified for high-end Northern Virginia projects.

Key Differences

Manufacturing tolerances

Techo-Bloc manufactures to tighter dimensional tolerances, meaning individual pavers vary less from piece to piece. This produces tighter joints and a more uniform finished appearance. Belgard's quality control is good but slightly more variable across their product range. In practice, the difference is subtle — you'd notice it if you were comparing side by side, but most homeowners aren't shopping at that level of detail.

Style range

Belgard has a wider overall product range — more price points, more sizes, more colors. If you're looking for something very specific or unusual, Belgard probably has it. Techo-Bloc focuses on a smaller, more curated product line at a consistently higher quality level.

Price

At comparable quality levels, Belgard and Techo-Bloc are similarly priced — roughly $18–$35 per sq ft installed for standard residential collections. Techo-Bloc's premium collections (Blu Grande large format, Raffinato, Libre) can push to $35–$50 per sq ft installed. Belgard's premium Urbana and Dimensions series are similarly positioned.

Warranty

Belgard offers a limited lifetime warranty when installed by an authorized contractor. Techo-Bloc offers a 25-year warranty on most products. In practice, quality pavers from either brand installed on a proper base in Northern Virginia's climate should last 30–50 years without significant issues.

Which Should You Choose?

Here's how I guide clients through this decision during consultations:

  • Traditional Colonial or brick home: Belgard Cambridge, Olde Towne, or Mega Arbel. Techo-Bloc Bel Air or Bel Classico. Either works beautifully with traditional architecture.
  • Transitional home (mixed traditional/modern): Both brands have strong transitional options. Belgard Mega Bergerac, Techo-Bloc Bel Air Modern.
  • Contemporary/modern home: Techo-Bloc Blu Grande or Raffinato (large format, very clean look). Belgard Urbana (similar aesthetic).
  • Budget-conscious project: Belgard's mid-range residential collections deliver excellent value. Techo-Bloc's entry-level products are comparable.
  • Premium project, highest quality: Both brands have collections that will satisfy the most discerning homeowner. Techo-Bloc's premium lines have a slight edge in refined aesthetics.

The honest answer: both brands make excellent pavers for Northern Virginia's climate. Don't agonize over the brand — focus on finding the specific product within either line that matches your home and your vision. That's where I spend my time with clients during the design phase.
